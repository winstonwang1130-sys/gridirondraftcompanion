Dallas Cowboys Fantasy Football Guide 2026: CeeDee Lamb's WR1 Case

Published May 21, 2026 · Dallas Cowboys · 7 min read

CeeDee Lamb remains a top-5 wide receiver despite quarterback uncertainty in Dallas. The Cowboys' pass-heavy offense ensures fantasy relevance for their skill players.

CeeDee Lamb — WR · ADP ~5 · Draft Range: Round 1

Lamb is a top-5 pick with 120+ target potential. He's quarterback-proof — he produced with Cooper Rush under center and will thrive regardless of who starts. Lock him in at the 1.05–1.08 range.

George Pickens — WR · ADP ~70 · Draft Range: Round 7–8

Pickens arrives in Dallas after a breakout in Pittsburgh. His deep-threat ability complements Lamb perfectly. Target as a WR3 with WR2 upside in the middle rounds.

Jake Ferguson — TE · ADP ~85 · Draft Range: Round 9–10

Ferguson is a safety-valve TE with 60+ catch potential. A late-round TE1 option if you punt the position.

Javonte Williams — RB · ADP ~95 · Draft Range: Round 10+

Williams leads a committee backfield in Dallas. Low-end RB3 with flex upside — don't reach, but consider in double-digit rounds.

Brandon Aubrey — K · ADP ~80 · Draft Range: Round 13

Aubrey is the most reliable kicker in fantasy. Draft in the final round for consistent 10+ points weekly.
