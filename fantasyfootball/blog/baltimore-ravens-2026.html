Baltimore Ravens Fantasy Football Guide 2026: Lamar's Dual-Threat Dominance

Published May 23, 2026 · Baltimore Ravens · 7 min read

Lamar Jackson and Derrick Henry form the most dangerous QB-RB duo in fantasy football. The Ravens' run-heavy offense creates consistent fantasy production across multiple positions.

Lamar Jackson — QB · ADP ~33 · Draft Range: Round 3–4

Jackson is a perennial top-5 QB thanks to his rushing dominance. 800+ rushing yards and 10+ rushing TDs give him a floor no other QB can match. Draft in Round 3 if you want to lock in QB early.

Derrick Henry — RB · ADP ~30 · Draft Range: Round 3

Henry defied age in 2025 with 1,400+ yards in Baltimore's offense. The TD vulture risk from Lamar exists, but Henry's volume and goal-line work make him a solid RB2 with RB1 upside.

Zay Flowers — WR · ADP ~62 · Draft Range: Round 6–7

Flowers is Lamar's top target but Baltimore's run-heavy scheme limits his ceiling. He's a WR3 with weekly WR2 upside — target in the middle rounds.

Mark Andrews — TE · ADP ~52 · Draft Range: Round 5–6

Andrews remains a red-zone threat despite sharing targets with Isaiah Likely. A viable TE1 if the top-tier TEs are gone.
