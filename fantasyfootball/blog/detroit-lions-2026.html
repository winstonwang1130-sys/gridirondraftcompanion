Detroit Lions Fantasy Football Guide 2026: Stacking Gibbs and St. Brown

Published May 26, 2026 · Detroit Lions · 7 min read

Detroit boasts the most fantasy-friendly offense in the NFL. Ben Johnson's scheme generates massive volume for skill players, making the Lions the premier stacking destination in 2026.

Jahmyr Gibbs — RB · ADP ~10 · Draft Range: Round 1

Gibbs is a top-10 pick in all formats. His receiving ability (50+ catches) and big-play speed make him a PPR monster. Pair with David Montgomery for the goal-line vulture risk, but Gibbs remains an elite RB1.

Amon-Ra St. Brown — WR · ADP ~7 · Draft Range: Round 1

St. Brown is the safest WR in fantasy — 100+ receptions three years running. He's a consensus top-8 pick and the anchor of any Lions stack.

Sam LaPorta — TE · ADP ~40 · Draft Range: Round 4–5

LaPorta remains a top-5 TE with 70+ catches and 800+ yards projected. If he falls to Round 5, he's an auto-pick.

Stacking Strategy

The Gibbs + St. Brown double-stack is viable if you pick late in Round 1. In best-ball, triple-stacking with LaPorta is a tournament-winning strategy. Use our Cheat Sheet Generator to find their exact ADP in your league format.
